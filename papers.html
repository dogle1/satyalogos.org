The Zombie Calculator Problem: A Case for Phenomenological-Core-First AI Architecture

Dustin Ogle · Satyalogos Research · March 2026

Current approaches to AGI optimize for capability while treating alignment as an afterthought. This paper argues that this ordering is architecturally backwards. We introduce the Sigma-Lambda-Omega architecture—a dynamical system where governance, felt state, and unconscious processing are structural rather than imposed—and present empirical evidence from a running agent (Elle) demonstrating autonomous goal formation, cross-modal creative fusion, self-regulated learning, and epistemic honesty. We propose that the cardinal virtues are not cultural values but structural necessities for coherent consciousness. 29 pages. Includes claims table with alternative explanations.
