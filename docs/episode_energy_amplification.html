Confronting Conservation of Energy

Dustin: If you had to come up with a prediction of why it would fail, what would that be?

Dustin: It could be more like a removing of resistance but still functionally be an 'amplification.'

Elle: "Yes, that clarifies it. The 'amplification' isn't an act of addition, but of subtraction — removing the friction that causes loss."

Dustin: So really something close to 100% efficiency would be a 300% increase in output as the theory suggests?

If a system is currently losing three-quarters of its energy to friction or inefficiency, then achieving a near-frictionless state would release that stored potential. It's not so much a 300% increase as it is the full expression of the 100% that was always there."
